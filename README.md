✈️ Airline Satisfaction Intelligence
Exploratory Data Analysis on 120,000+ Passenger Records

📌 Overview
This project dives deep into the satisfaction patterns of 120,000+ airline passengers to answer one core business question:

What actually makes an airline passenger satisfied — and what doesn't?

Through structured Exploratory Data Analysis, this project segments passengers by travel type, customer type, gender, and flight class to uncover hidden patterns, pain points, and actionable insights that airlines can act on.

🎯 Objectives

Identify the key drivers of passenger satisfaction and dissatisfaction
Analyse how satisfaction varies across first-time vs. returning travelers
Understand why personal travelers report significantly lower satisfaction
Determine which service dimensions matter most to passengers
Translate findings into clear, business-ready recommendations


🔍 Key Findings

🔴 75%+ of first-time flyers were dissatisfied — primarily due to poor Online Boarding and In-flight WiFi experience
📶 In-flight WiFi had the strongest correlation with overall satisfaction (r = 0.35) — outranking food, cleanliness, and seat comfort
✈️ Personal travelers showed significantly lower satisfaction compared to business travelers, driven by departure/arrival delays and poor leg room
💺 Business class passengers consistently reported higher satisfaction, especially on long-distance routes
📱 Digital experience factors (WiFi, Online Boarding, Ease of Booking) emerged as the new frontier of passenger satisfaction

📊 Analysis Workflow
Raw Data → Data Cleaning → Feature Encoding → EDA → Visualization → Insights → Business Recommendations

Data Cleaning — Handled null values, duplicates, and data type corrections
Feature Engineering — Encoded 5+ categorical variables (Gender, Class, Travel Type, Customer Type)
Univariate Analysis — Distribution plots for satisfaction, class, and travel type
Bivariate & Segmented Analysis — Cross-group comparisons using groupby, pie charts, and bar plots
Correlation Analysis — Heatmaps and bar charts to rank satisfaction drivers
KDE Analysis — Density plots to identify discomfort clusters among dissatisfied segments
Business Reporting — Findings translated into a 10-slide executive presentation


💡 Business Recommendations

Fix Digital First — Invest in Online Booking UX, Boarding systems, and In-flight WiFi infrastructure
Target First-Time Flyers — Onboarding guides and simplified digital touchpoints can significantly improve their experience
Address Delay Communication — Personal travelers are acutely sensitive to delays; proactive communication can soften dissatisfaction
Upgrade Economy Leg Room — A consistent pain point that is fixable with minor cabin reconfigurations
Segment Marketing by Travel Type — Business and personal travelers have fundamentally different expectations; one-size-fits-all service won't work
